# Buprenorphine rotation

Clinical-workaround family. Switching long-term full-agonist patients to buprenorphine; more a safer-opioid strategy than a true anti-tolerance one, but a real deployable arm.

## What it is

Switch long-term full-agonist opioid patients to buprenorphine, a partial mu-agonist with high receptor affinity, possible antihyperalgesic properties, and a different safety/pharmacology profile from full agonists. The logic is the same incomplete-cross-tolerance workaround as other rotation: patients who respond poorly to one opioid may respond better to another.

## Human evidence

A 2021 systematic review of 22 studies found low-quality evidence that buprenorphine rotation reduced pain without precipitating withdrawal or serious adverse events. ([JAMA Network][1])

## Why it failed / what would change the verdict

*Not a failure; the evidence is just thin and low-quality. What would change the verdict: a properly powered rotation trial on durability endpoints rather than the current low-to-very-low-quality observational base. Practically, buprenorphine avoids methadone's worst liabilities (QTc, treacherous conversion ratios), so it deserves equal billing as an ambulatory-safer arm.*

## Verdict

**Live ember.** Potentially practical, especially in the chronic-pain/OUD overlap, but evidence quality is low and it is more a safer-opioid strategy than anti-tolerance. A real, deployable arm for the same pilot as methadone rotation.
