# Ibudilast

The strongest live candidate in the glial/TLR4/neuroinflammation branch: plausible biology and decades of overseas safety data, but thin human translation.

## What it is

A glial/PDE/TLR4-ish modulator used in Japan and Korea for other indications (the same molecule as AV411/MN-166). The neuroimmune rationale: chronic opioids recruit glial and inflammatory signaling that opposes analgesia and contributes to tolerance, dependence, and OIH-like sensitization.

## Human evidence

The best human signal is a small lab study in 11 opioid-dependent male volunteers, where ibudilast may enhance oxycodone analgesia and may be useful for OUD-related outcomes; broader validation is lacking. ([Nature][1])

*Add: note that this is an abuse-liability paradigm, not durable cancer-pain analgesia, and that there is no cancer-pain data.*

## Why it failed / what would change the verdict

*Not a failure; under-tested in the relevant population. "Glia matter" is not enough on its own. What would change the verdict: a sharply selected OIH/escalation trial with tolerance-specific endpoints, a named investigator, and an adoption path, rather than another broad trial.*

## Verdict

**Watchlist.** Mechanistically interesting, medium-low tractability. Maybe fundable if a specific investigator has a clean small trial design; not a cheap generic that works everywhere.

[1]: https://www.nature.com/articles/npp201770 "Effects of Ibudilast on the Subjective, Reinforcing, and Analgesic Effects"
