# Amantadine, magnesium, dexmedetomidine, pregabalin, NSAID adjuncts

Acute perioperative OIH-prevention branch. Real, measurable, and cheap, but aimed at a different population and outcome window than chronic cancer-pain tolerance.

## What it is

A cluster targeting acute opioid-induced hyperalgesia and postoperative opioid exposure: dexmedetomidine, clonidine, amantadine, magnesium, pregabalin, NSAIDs, and related adjuncts. The aim is to reduce central sensitization or opioid requirements around surgery, especially when high-potency short-acting opioids such as remifentanil are used.

## Human evidence

A 2023 network meta-analysis of 33 RCTs / 1,711 patients found several interventions associated with lower postoperative pain after opioid-based anesthesia; amantadine ranked best for pain, while dexmedetomidine was the only intervention that outperformed placebo across all indicators. ([Frontiers][1])

## Why it failed / what would change the verdict

*Not a failure; a scope mismatch. This is acute postoperative OIH prevention, not chronic analgesic-tolerance prevention: population, outcome window, and implementation context all differ from the "cancer pain starts wearing off" problem. What would promote it: evidence that any of these adjuncts preserves analgesia over time in chronic palliative opioid use, not just around surgery.*

## Verdict

**Adjunct.** Real but narrow. A clinical-guideline-optimization angle and a useful sidebar; should not be allowed to masquerade as the main anti-tolerance solution.
