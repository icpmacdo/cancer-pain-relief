# Amantadine, magnesium, dexmedetomidine, pregabalin, NSAID adjuncts

This candidate cluster sits in the acute perioperative OIH prevention branch of the landscape. It is more clinical than many anti-tolerance ideas, but it is also narrower than the chronic cancer-pain problem that motivates the report.

## What it is

Perioperative anti-OIH/opioid-sparing drugs, many already cheap/generic.

A separate cluster of interventions targets acute opioid-induced hyperalgesia and postoperative opioid exposure: dexmedetomidine, clonidine, amantadine, magnesium, pregabalin, NSAIDs, and related adjuncts. The idea is to reduce central sensitization or opioid requirements around surgery, especially when high-potency short-acting opioids such as remifentanil are used.

## Human evidence

A 2023 network meta-analysis of 33 RCTs / 1,711 patients found several interventions associated with lower postoperative pain after opioid-based anesthesia; amantadine ranked best for pain, while dexmedetomidine was the only intervention that outperformed placebo across all indicators. ([Frontiers][1])

This evidence base is more clinical than many anti-tolerance ideas. A 2023 network meta-analysis of pharmacologic interventions for postoperative OIH found signals for several drugs, with dexmedetomidine standing out across multiple indicators. That is real.

## Tractability verdict

**Real but narrow.** This is acute postoperative OIH prevention, not chronic opioid-tolerance prevention. Could be a "clinical guideline optimization" angle, not the core bounty.

Preventing acute postoperative OIH is not the same as preventing chronic analgesic tolerance in cancer or palliative-care patients. It may reduce postoperative pain or opioid consumption, but the affected population, outcome window, and implementation context differ from the "cancer pain starts wearing off" problem that motivates this report.

This could still be worth mentioning as a tractable adjacent area: perioperative protocols are measurable, guidelines can change, and the endpoints are clearer than in chronic palliative pain. But it should not be allowed to masquerade as the main anti-tolerance solution.
